I am a master's student in the Statistics programme at the University of Copenhagen. Currently, I am writing my master's thesis concluding my studies early next year. The topic is the application of a general time-varying branching process in phylogenetics/phylodynamics and is supervised by [Samir Bhatt](https://publichealth.ku.dk/about-the-department/section-epidemiology/?pure=en/persons/707469) and [Carsten Wiuf](https://www.math.ku.dk/english/staff/?pure=en/persons/156899).

## Education
In 2017 I started my bachelor's degree in mathematics at the University of Copenhagen. Not being able to choose between studying physics, computer science or neuroscience I chose mathematics because I saw some of [Susanne Ditlevsen](http://web.math.ku.dk/~susanne/)'s work which just sounded so cool and touched upon all these topics. In my very first course on probability and statistics by [Helle Sørensen](https://www.math.ku.dk/english/staff/?pure=en/persons/141330) I fell in love with uncertainty and models. Since then, I delved into the deep theory of probability and its use in statistics.

I visited University of California, Berkeley for a semester in 2020, where I discovered more computational approaches to statistics, which have since interested me very much. When I got home the pandemic was raging and I had to write my bachelor's thesis, so going into epidemic modeling was a low-hanging fruit for me. My thesis was entitled *Modelling COVID-19 with a realistic population network model: Will New York achieve herd immunity?* and touched on classical and simulation intensive epidemic modeling with a focus on population heterogeneity and network structures. It was supervised by Susanne Ditlevsen and heavily inspired by work of [Tom Britton](https://staff.math.su.se/tom.britton/).

When I in 2021 started my master's program in statistics (also at UCPH), stochastic dynamics of biology shaped my way through the education. I have done courses and several independent projects in advanced probability theory, stochastic processses, computational statistics and mathematical biology. In 2023 I joined Samir Bhatt's group, Computational and Mathematical Global Health, at the Section of Epidemiology where I have since used my time studying branching processes and their use as models for epidemics and phylogenies.

## Teaching
Since 2019 I have TA'ed in 12 courses, most of them have been introductory statistical courses for biologist and medical students, but I have also taught on more pure measure theory and probability courses, a course in mathematical statistics and one on mathematical epidemiology. I always look forward to teaching, and I hope that some of my efforts have made these beauty of these subjects accessible to my students. Currently I teach [Statistical Data Analysis 1](https://kurser.ku.dk/course/LMAB10069U) and in a bit also [Probability Theory](https://kurser.ku.dk/course/NMAB21015U).

## Work
Besides teaching I worked for three years at the [Section of Biostatistics](https://biostat.ku.dk/), where I tested the waters with my abilities in practical data analysis with medical and pharmacological applications. I enjoyed it very much and learned a lot about mixed-effect models, survival analysis, semi-parametric inference and working with huge amounts of data, but realised that my primary interest is in mathematical biology, not statistics per se. Before this I worked as a local representative at UCPH for [IDA](https://ida.dk/) a trade-union for natural science academics and students, I did a lot of outreach as well as planning of big events. This job learned me a lot about talking to large gatherings and gave me many organizational tools. I have also had a few stints as a chef at a hotel in Skagen, kicking off my great passion for food and drinks.
