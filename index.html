Frederik Mølkjær Andersen, PhD Fellow

I'm a PhD student at the Section for Health Data Science and AI at the University of Copenhagen. My work focuses on using mathematical models to better understand biological and health-related systems. I mostly work with stochastic processes and computational methods, aiming to connect theoretical models with the real world.

At the moment I work a lot with:

  • Stochastic trees and branching processes
  • Mathematical phylogenetics
  • Inference from stochastic processes

More broadly, I'm interested in:

  • Probability theory and stochastic processes
  • Computational statistics
  • Evolution and phylogenetics
  • Infectious disease epidemiology
  • Science and math communication
